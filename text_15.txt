The Storm

The sky turned bruised and purple-black,
As wind came howling on the attack.
It tore the leaves from trembling trees,
And whipped the whitecaps on the seas.

The rain came down in sheets of grey,
Washing the dust of summer away.

The birds fell silent, the dogs did whine,
Hiding away until the sun would shine.
But in the lighthouse, steady and bright,
A beam of hope cut through the night.
